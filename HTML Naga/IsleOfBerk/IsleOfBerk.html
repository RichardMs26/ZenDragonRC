Isle of Berk

Long ago, before the first Viking set sail, the world was filled with magic and mythical creatures. Berkonir, the god of northern winds and seas, felt lonely and wanted to create a sanctuary in the mortal world. One day, he saw young dragons playing in the waves and was moved by their joy. Berkonir summoned a great storm and raised an island from the sea. He named the island Berk, with towering mountains, dense forests, and clear streams. He made this island a paradise for dragons and other creatures, with favorable winds and seas rich with fish.

Location

Isle of Berk map

The Isle of Berk, a rugged and remote island, is located far in the Northern seas, characterized by its dramatic cliffs and rolling hills. Known for its harsh weather and resilient Viking inhabitants, Berk is a place of great adventure and mythical creatures, most notably dragons. This isolated yet vibrant island thrives on its rich traditions, close-knit community, and the unique bond between its people and dragons, making it a haven of harmony and bravery amidst the fierce elements of the North.
